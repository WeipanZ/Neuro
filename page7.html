Dementia

Dementia is a term used to describe brain diseases that causes one's mental ability to decline. Symptoms of dementia include memory loss, losing the ability to perform everyday tasks, communication difficulties, losing the ability to focus and pay attention, improper reasoning and judgement, and a reduction in visual perception. The most common types of dementia is Alzheimer's disease, which makes up 60 to 80 percent of cases.
